ABOUT BIS

The Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) is India’s national standards body, functioning under the Ministry of Consumer Affairs, Food & Public Distribution. Established under the BIS Act, 2016, it is responsible for formulating, certifying, and enforcing quality and safety standards for various products and services. BIS plays a crucial role in consumer protection, industrial development, and international trade by ensuring compliance with globally recognized benchmarks. It provides certifications such as the ISI mark for industrial and consumer goods, the Hallmark for gold and silver jewelry, and the Compulsory Registration Scheme (CRS) for electronic products. Additionally, BIS conducts product testing, quality assessments, and market surveillance to uphold reliability and safety standards.

Awareness of BIS standards is essential to fostering a culture of quality and compliance among manufacturers and consumers. The organization actively engages in training programs, workshops, and awareness campaigns to educate stakeholders on the benefits of standardization. BIS certification enhances consumer trust, promotes business credibility, and ensures safer products in the market. Through initiatives like the Standards Promotion & Consumer Affairs Department, BIS disseminates information via advertisements, digital outreach, and collaborations with educational institutions to instill awareness about quality assurance among students, professionals, and businesses.

Despite these efforts, widespread awareness and adoption of BIS certification remain a challenge. Strengthening public outreach through enhanced digital campaigns, integrating standardization into educational curricula, and fostering industry collaborations can improve compliance and consumer awareness. Government support, combined with industry-led initiatives, can further encourage the use of BIS-certified products. By increasing awareness and accessibility, BIS can help ensure product safety, enhance consumer confidence, and boost India’s manufacturing sector, aligning it with global quality standards.
